Dr. Norman Borlaug

Here's a walk-through of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • March 25, 1914- Saude, Iowa, U.S.
  • 1937-1942 Borlaug received his B.S in forestry inand PhD in plant pathology and genetics from the University of Minnesota
  • 1940–1969Borlaug led the introduction of these high yielding varieties combined with modern agricultural production techniques to Mexico, Pakistan, and India.
  • 1942-1944 - Borlaug was employed as a microbiologist at DuPont in Wilmington, Delaware. It was planned that he would lead research on industrial and agricultural bacteriocides, fungicides, and preservatives.
  • 1964 - Borlaug was made the director of the International Wheat Improvement Program at El Batán, Texcoco, on the eastern fringes of Mexico City, as part of the newly established Consultative Group on International Agricultural Research's International Maize and Wheat Improvement Center (Centro Internacional de Mejoramiento de Maíz y Trigo, or CIMMYT).
  • 1979 - Borlaug retired officially from the position, but remained a CIMMYT senior consultant. In addition to taking up charitable and educational roles, he continued to be involved in plant research at CIMMYT with wheat, triticale, barley, maize, and high-altitude sorghum.
  • 1981 - Borlaug became a founding member of the World Cultural Council,
  • 1984 - Borlaug began teaching and conducting research at Texas A&M University. Eventually he was given the title Distinguished Professor International Agriculture at the university the holder of the Eugene Butler Endowed Chair in Agricultural Biotechnology.
  • September 2009 - Borlaug served on the faculty of the University of Minnesota, University of lowa, Cornell University, and Texas A&M University. Borlaug remained at A&M until his death.
